Kūboku Takaku and his adopted daughter Hisako bridged the gap between traditional, artisanal craft and modern art in a way that is immediate and beautiful. Using a traditional dyeing technique, roketsuzome, they produced the objects they are most famous for: kimono and obi that are elegant, sensitively designed, and deeply rooted in Japan's rich history and spirituality. This shimmering gold kimono was made by Kūboku in the middle of his career, not too long after he transitioned from working in fine arts circles to designing wearable works of art for the Tokyo elite.
