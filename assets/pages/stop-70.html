To represent summer, Hisako created this obi with a stylized water current design. It is the complement to her pale pink kimono adorned with swathes of blue poppies. Looking at both the kimono and obi, you can almost feel a cooling breeze. The effervescent water current design of this obi was inspired by Kūboku's obi of the same name. It provides us with an excellent opportunity to see how much these two artists share - in technique, style, and philosophy - and what distinguishes each. Here, Hisako's colors are warmer and her forms more looped and playful. Still she maintains the essential balance of opposites with the stronger gold lines playing off the more delicate silver strokes.
